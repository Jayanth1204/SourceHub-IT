Virat Kohli is a legendary Indian cricketer widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, he rose to fame after leading India to victory in the 2008 ICC U-19 Cricket World Cup. He made his international debut in the same year and quickly established himself as a key player across all formats. Known for his aggressive style, unmatched consistency, and fitness, Kohli has broken numerous batting records, including being among the fastest to score 8,000, 10,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). He captained the Indian team from 2017 to 2022, leading them to major Test series victories, including a historic win in Australia. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), he has been a cornerstone of the Royal Challengers Bangalore franchise and holds the record for the most runs in the tournament’s history. Off the field, he is a global sports icon, a successful entrepreneur, and a philanthropist through his Virat Kohli Foundation. Kohli is married to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, and together they are one of India’s most influential celebrity couples.
